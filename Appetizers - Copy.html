Tokwat Baboy

Generally, Tokwat Baboy is considered as an appetizer or a side dish. Most people like to have this with their favorite beverage and enjoy it as an appetizer, while others love to have it with rice porridge such as Arroz Caldo, Goto, or lugaw.

How To Cook Tokwat Baboy

Servings:
  • 6
Equipment:
  • 1 medium to large pan or pot
Ingredients:
  • 1 lb pig ears
  • 1 lb pork belly liempo
  • 1 lb tofu extra firm tofu
  • 1 tablespoon whole peppercorn
  • 1 piece onion sliced
  • 3 Thai chili pepper chopped
  • 2 stalks scallions cut in 1/2 inch length
  • 5 cups water
  • 2 cups cooking oil
Vinegar sauce ingredients
  • 1 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
Instructions:
  1. Pour-in water in a pot and bring to a boil
    5 cups water
  2. Add salt and whole peppercorn
    1 tablespoon whole peppercorn, 1/2 teaspoon salt
  3. Put-in the pig’s ears and pork belly then simmer until tender (about 30 mins to 1 hour)
    1 lb pig ears, 1 lb pork belly
  4. Pour cooking oil on a separate pan and allow to heat
    2 cups cooking oil
  5. When the oil is hot enough, deep-fry the tofu until color turns golden brown and outer texture is somewhat crispy
    1 lb tofu
  6. Cube the fried tofu and slice the boiled pig’s ears and pork belly into bite-sized pieces then set aside
    1 lb pig ears, 1 lb tofu, 1 lb pork belly
  7. Combine the vinegar sauce ingredients starting with sugar, salt, soy sauce, and vinegar. Stir.
    1/4 cup soy sauce, 1 1/2 teaspoons sugar, 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1 1/2 cup white vinegar
  8. Microwave for 1 minute.
  9. Add the ground black pepper, onions, green onions, and chili pepper. Transfer the sauce in a serving bowl.
    1 piece onion, 2 stalks scallions, 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper, 3 Thai chili pepper
  10. Place the sliced meat and tofu in a serving plate with the bowl of sauce by the side
  11. Serve hot! Share and Enjoy!
